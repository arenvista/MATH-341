# LU Factorization

## Example

Find the LU Factoriazation of:

$$
	A = \begin{bmatrix}
		2 & 2 & 3  \\
		5 & 9 & 10 \\
		4 & 1 & 2
	\end{bmatrix}
$$

Goal:

$$
	A =
	\begin{array}
		  & \begin{bmatrix}
			    1 & 0 & 0 \\
			    x & 1 & 0 \\
			    x & x & 1
		    \end{bmatrix}
		  & \begin{bmatrix}
			    x & x & x \\
			    0 & x & x \\
			    0 & 0 & x
		    \end{bmatrix} \\
		L & U
	\end{array}
$$

Where $x$ represents any $x \in \mathbb{R}$.

Now row reduct to get $U$.

- Record the coefficents of each row operation, these are called $k$ values.

$$
	A = \begin{bmatrix}
		2 & 2 & 3  \\
		5 & 9 & 10 \\
		4 & 1 & 2
	\end{bmatrix} \rightarrow (R_2 - \frac{5}{2}R_1) \land (R_3-2R_1)
	\rightarrow \begin{bmatrix}
		2 & 2  & 3           \\
		0 & 4  & \frac{2}{2} \\
		0 & -3 & -4
	\end{bmatrix}
$$

Observe the row operations:

$$
	(R_3-2R_1) \land (R_2 - \frac{5}{2}R_1) \implies \{ k_{1,1}=1, k_{1,2}=\frac{5}{2}, k_{1,3}=2 \} \\
$$

Thus we find the first column of L:

$$
	L =
	\begin{bmatrix}
		1           & ? & ? \\
		\frac{5}{2} & ? & ? \\
		2           & ? & ?
	\end{bmatrix}
$$

Thus we can generalize the earlier expression:

$$
	A =
	\begin{array}
		  & \begin{bmatrix}
			    1       & 0       & 0 \\
			    k_{1,2} & 1       & 0 \\
			    k_{1,3} & k_{2,3} & 1
		    \end{bmatrix}
		  & \begin{bmatrix}
			    x_{1,1} & x_{2,1} & x_{3,1} \\
			    0       & x_{2,2} & x_{3,2} \\
			    0       & 0       & x_{3,3}
		    \end{bmatrix} \\
		L & U
	\end{array}
$$
